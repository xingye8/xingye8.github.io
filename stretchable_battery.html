Stretchable Battery

Mechanically Rupturing Liquid Metal Oxide Induces Electrochemical Energy

Liquid metal and its oxide

Gallium-based liquid metal forms a thin layer of surface oxide when exposed to oxygen. Even if the oxide is damaged, it quickly re-forms spontaneously.

We discovered a new method of manipulating liquid metal oxide by using it as an electrochemical energy source.

A new way to generate energy

Rupturing liquid metal oxide converts chemical energy to electrical energy. A needle repeatedly scratched the surface of the liquid metal in one of the chambers to break its oxide skin. The chemical energy of its re-oxidation reaction is partially converted to electrical energy.

Stretchable battery with liquid metal

The new battery design includes rigid wires embedded in a liquid metal emulsion and is packaged by silicone rubber. Stretching the soft device ruptures liquid metal oxide and generates voltage.

LIVE DEMO

The stretchable battery is capable of powering a LED. 

Self-powered keyboard

This liquid metal-based keyboard works without external power. This device responds to compression and generates voltage. A microcontroller processes the voltage signal and sends it to the computer for display.

A sustainable future

The power generated from liquid metal is reversible. The battery delivers power on demand and remains dormant at rest. This technology is particularly suitable for low-power applications, such as strain-activated electronic switches, vibration-sensitive detection systems, and hibernating devices that can be woken up by external mechanical events.

This work is published on Advanced Functional Materials:

X. Ye, Z. Zheng, J. G. Werner, J. W. Boley, Mechanically Rupturing Liquid Metal Oxide Induces Electrochemical Energy. Adv. Funct. Mater. 2024, 34, 2309177.
