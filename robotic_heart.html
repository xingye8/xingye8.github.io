Soft Robotic Heart

Cardiac model is an important tool for doctors to visualize their patients' hearts.
We developed our version of soft robotic heart based on innovative technologies of cardiac assist device [1] and soft origami-style actuator [2].

[1] E. T. Roche, et al. Soft robotic sleeve supports heart function. Sci. Transl. Med. 2017;9:eaaf3925.
[2] S. Li, et al. Fluid-driven origami-inspired artificial muscles. Proc. Natl. Acad. Sci. USA. 2017;114:13132-13137.

3D Heart Model

The structure of the human heart was provided by BodyParts3D, an open-source anatomy database.
It serves as the structural basis of our silicone rubber-based soft robotic heart.

Fabrication Steps

Slicing + 3D Printing

We used Geomagic to segregate the atriums and ventricles, and 3D-printed the heart wall structure with PLA material.

Casting Silicone Rubber

We constructed internal and external molds of the heart structure with alginate powder, and then cure Ecoflex silicone rubber against the customized molds.

McKibben PAM

The classic pneumatic artificial muscles (PAM) can contract up to 40% of their original lengths.

Robotic Heart Actuated By PAMs

In addition to the PAM-based heart model, we also built a vacuum-powered soft robotic cardiac simulator.

Circular actuator

The vacuum-driven soft actuator comprises of a nylon-based zigzag-style skeleton and a piece of PVC thin membrane. By forming a circular shape, the diameter of the actuator would shrink when actuated by negative pneumatic pressures.
